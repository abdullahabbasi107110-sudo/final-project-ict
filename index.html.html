Our Story

The Taste of Peshawar
in the Heart of Karachi

Baithak Peshawari has been a cornerstone of Boat Basin's food scene, bringing authentic Pashtun cuisine to Karachi's Clifton neighbourhood. Known for its rustic low wooden seating, charpais under the open sky, and hearty flavours, Baithak is more than a restaurant — it is a cultural experience.

From the signature Chapli Kebab sizzling in ghee to the crispy Halwa Puri served at dawn, every dish tells a story of Peshawar's grand culinary heritage. The restaurant features both open-air outdoor seating and an air-conditioned family hall — welcoming all of Karachi, one plate at a time.

What Makes Us Unforgettable

Peshawari Karahi

Slow-cooked mutton or chicken karahi with tomatoes, fresh ginger, and whole green chillies — a Baithak signature that draws crowds every evening.

Famous Nashta

Karachi wakes up to Baithak's Halwa Puri — crispy fried puris, sweet semolina halwa, qeema, and piping hot chai. A beloved morning tradition.

Our Heritage

About Baithak

Our Story

From Peshawar's Streets to Karachi's Heart

Baithak Peshawari was born from a single dream — to bring the warmth, generosity, and bold flavours of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa's culinary tradition to the people of Karachi. Nestled in the iconic Boat Basin food street of Clifton, Baithak has been serving its community for over two decades.

The name "Baithak" — meaning a gathering place — reflects the restaurant's soul. It is a space where families gather over steaming karahis, where friends bond on charpais over chai, and where every stranger becomes a guest. The outdoor open-air seating under Karachi's night sky, paired with the warmth of traditional Peshawari hospitality, creates an atmosphere impossible to find anywhere else.

The menu is a love letter to Peshawar: Chapli Kebabs fried in pure animal fat for authentic flavour, slow-cooked Karahis in heavy iron woks, crispy Halwa Puri nashta available round the clock, and stuffed parathas that draw breakfast lovers from across the city. Every dish is prepared to order, with fresh spices and time-honoured recipes.

❧ ❧ ❧
Late 1990s
Baithak Peshawari opens its doors at Boat Basin, Clifton — initially a small nashta setup serving Halwa Puri and Qeema Paratha to early morning regulars.
Early 2000s
The BBQ and Karahi menu launches, drawing evening crowds. Chapli Kebab becomes the restaurant's most talked-about signature dish across Karachi food circles.
2010s
An air-conditioned family hall is added, expanding capacity. Baithak is featured in Youlin Magazine as a beloved Karachi institution for authentic Peshawari food.
Today
Rated 4.8 stars, serving over 1,000 guests daily. Baithak continues to operate 24 hours, 7 days a week — a true Karachi landmark for over two decades.
